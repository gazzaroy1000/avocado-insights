Fun Facts About Avocados


1. Avocados Are Actually Fruits!

  • Though often used in savory dishes, avocados are technically large berries with a single seed.

2. Over 2,000 Varieties of Avocados Exist
  • The most popular type is the Hass avocado, but there are many more varieties, including Fuerte, Bacon, and Zutano.

3. Avocados Are Nutrient Powerhouses
  • Avocados are packed with over 20 vitamins and minerals, including potassium, folate, vitamin E, and healthy fats.

4. Avocado Trees Can't Self-Pollinate
  • They need another avocado tree nearby to produce fruit, making avocado farming an intricate process.

5. They Were Once Called "Alligator Pears"
  • Due to their bumpy green skin and pear-like shape, early English speakers referred to avocados as "alligator pears."

6. Avocados Are Perfect for Your Heart
  • They are rich in monounsaturated fats, which help lower bad cholesterol and promote heart health.

7. They Ripen Faster in a Paper Bag
  • If you're eager to eat your avocado, placing it in a paper bag with a banana or apple will speed up the ripening process.

8. Avocados Have Been Around for 10,000 Years
  • Fossil evidence shows that humans have been enjoying avocados for over 10,000 years.

10. Avocados Were Considered a Luxury in the Past
